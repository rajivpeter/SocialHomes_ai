## 5. Key Regulations Driving HMS Demand

### Awaab's Law (Social Housing (Regulation) Act 2023)
Phased implementation of mandatory repair timeframes:

| Phase | Date | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | **27 October 2025** | Damp & mould + all emergency hazards |
| Phase 2 | **2026** | Excess cold/heat, falls, structural collapse, fire, electrical, hygiene hazards |
| Phase 3 | **2027** | All remaining HHSRS hazards (except overcrowding) |

Social landlords must investigate hazards within 14 days, begin repairs within 7 days of investigation, and complete emergency repairs within 24 hours. Failure to comply means tenants can claim compensation.

### Tenant Satisfaction Measures (TSMs)
- Mandatory since April 2023 for all registered providers with 1,000+ homes
- Annual collection and reporting of 22 defined metrics (12 perception-based, 10 management data)
- Covers: overall satisfaction, repairs, safety, complaints, ASB, neighbourhood management
- **Updated March 2026** to align with Awaab's Law responsive repair categories
- Results are published publicly, enabling benchmarking and regulatory scrutiny

### Housing Ombudsman Complaint Handling Code
- **Became statutory from 1 April 2024** (previously voluntary)
- Stage 1 response: 10 working days
- Stage 2 response: 20 working days
- 94% of complaints now responded to within the Code's timeframes (up from ~87% in 2023/24)
- Landlords must have a dedicated complaints policy and self-assess annually

### RSH Consumer Standards (from 1 April 2024)
Four new consumer standards that RSH can now proactively enforce:
1. **Safety and Quality Standard** - homes must be safe, well-maintained, decent
2. **Transparency, Influence and Accountability Standard** - tenant engagement and TSM reporting
3. **Neighbourhood and Community Standard** - estate management and ASB
4. **Tenancy Standard** - fair allocation and tenancy management

RSH has begun a programme of landlord inspections. New Competence and Conduct requirements expected from **October 2026**, plus proactive publication requirements.

**Why this drives HMS demand:** All of these regulations require robust data tracking, reporting, audit trails, and tenant communication capabilities that legacy systems often cannot deliver.

Sources:

## 1. AWAAB'S LAW

**Background:**
- Named after Awaab Ishak, a 2-year-old who died in December 2020 from a severe respiratory condition caused by prolonged exposure to mould in his social housing home in Rochdale
- His parents had complained repeatedly for three years; the social landlord took no action
- Enacted via **Section 42 of the Social Housing (Regulation) Act 2023**, which inserts Section 10A into the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985
- Section 10A received Royal Assent on 20 September 2023; the operational regulations were laid before Parliament on 25 June 2025

**Commencement Date:**
- **27 October 2025** (Phase 1)

**Mandated Timescales:**
- **Investigation of hazards:** Within **10 working days** of landlord becoming aware
- **Written summary to tenant:** Within **3 working days** of investigation concluding
- **Emergency hazards (investigation + action):** Within **24 hours**
- **Relevant safety work (non-emergency):** Must begin within **5 working days** of investigation conclusion
- **Long-stop for supplementary/preventative work:** Must physically begin within **12 weeks** if cannot start in 5 days
- **Alternative accommodation:** Must be provided if landlord cannot complete safety work within 5 working days (or 24 hours for emergencies)

**Phased Rollout:**
- **Phase 1 (27 Oct 2025):** Damp & mould (significant hazards) + all emergency hazards
- **Phase 2 (2026):** Excess cold/heat, falls, structural collapse, fire, electrical/explosions, hygiene hazards
- **Phase 3 (2027):** All remaining HHSRS hazards (excluding overcrowding)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- RSH can impose **unlimited fines** (previously capped at GBP 5,000 -- cap removed by the 2023 Act)
- RSH can issue enforcement notices and require Performance Improvement Plans
- RSH can authorise emergency remedial action at the landlord's expense
- RSH can remove officers of Registered Providers
- The "serious detriment test" has been removed -- RSH no longer needs to prove risk of serious detriment before acting
- Tenants can take **direct legal action through the courts** for breach of contract
- Housing Ombudsman can order repairs, award compensation, and require practice improvements

**Sources:**
- [GOV.UK -- Awaab's Law Guidance for Social Landlords](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/awaabs-law-guidance-for-social-landlords/awaabs-law-guidance-for-social-landlords-timeframes-for-repairs-in-the-social-rented-sector)
